Portfolio

In-Development Titles

  • Rift World

    Rift World is a fast-paced, hardcore FPS being created by Cerulean Interactive, and for which I have been brought on to perform various tasks related to polishing the game for release. At this point in development, my principal duties revolve around bug fixes, performance optimization, and gamepad support implementation.

  • Payload

    Payload is a cerebral, narrative-driven, 3D puzzle game being developed by Black Banshee Studios for PC. Throughout it's development I have filled many roles, including creative director, principal designer, narrative designer, UI designer, gameplay programmer, graphics programmer, UI programmer, and tools programmer (I wrote the puzzle solver by which the level/puzzle designers plugged in values to determine whether or not a level was beatable, and if so, how many and what solutions it had).

Released Titles

  • Squirgle

    For Planar Gazer's first title, I was responsible for all aspects of game development. Leveraging some of the LibGDX framework's primitive screen drawing capabilities, I came up with and implemented the graphics generation process by which visuals are constructed and displayed to the player; I designed and programmed all game logic and UI elements, as well as those algorithms by which the game's distinct and theoretically infinite "shape within shape" aesthetic is made possible; I oversaw QA for the title, and referenced its findings for design-allowed adjustments to gameplay, usability and tutorialization. Additionally, I composed all of the music within the game.

  • The Loneliest Artist

    The Loneliest Artist is a game for change developed by CelleC Games for Windows PCs, and for which I served as a gameplay/puzzle programmer. Specifically, I wrote the implementation for the hidden object puzzles encountered in the game, as well as for the majority of the player character's input responses and associated behaviors. Additionally, I scripted most of the game's scripted events.

  • Little Critters

    Little Critters is a Tamagotchi-style game developed by CelleC Games for iOS and Android, and for which I served as an AI and gameplay programmer. In short, I programmed the eponymous critters' non-UI-related behaviors, as well as implemented their softbody physics and item interactions.

  • Color Hopper

    Color Hopper is an educational game developed by CelleC Games for iOS and Android, and for which I served as a gameplay and UI programmer. In so doing, I coded much of the game's menu behaviors, as well as a significant portion of the player controller and player character behaviors.

  • Shape Hopper

    An extension of Color Hopper, Shape Hopper is yet another educational title developed by CelleC Games for iOS and Android. Although I did not work on this title directly, the code base I wrote for the previous entry in the Hopper anthology (Color Hopper) was used as a starting point for this title's development.

Game Jam Titles

  • Sense

    Sense is a 3D, 1st-person horror/exploration game I helped make for 2021's Global Game Jam. For this project, I handled all of the graphics programming and the vast majority of gameplay programming.

Interests

Principle examples of the sorts of titles I seek out include The Stanley Parable, Silent Hill 2, Keep Talking and Nobody Explodes, Hackmud, and Deus Ex, to name just a few.

In addition to video games, I thoroughly enjoy cooking, listening to and composing a wide range of music, and watching what I'm told are glacially slow films. My movie tastes span many genres, though I've a predilection towards horror, experimental, and well-choreographed action flicks. When I'm fortunate enough to have the time and money to do so, I also love traveling and staving off the boredom of a flight with a good bit of philosophy, or any sort of text that centers around the human condition.
